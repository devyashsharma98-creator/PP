## Design Context

### Users
- Pragya Pravah ERP serves a mixed audience: senior organisers and intellectual stakeholders, daily internal operators, and first-time public visitors evaluating the organisation through the homepage and demo flow.
- Public visitors need fast clarity on what the organisation is, why its work matters now, and how to explore or connect.
- Internal users need role-aware operational clarity across dashboard, aalekh, prachar, calendar, and related workflows without losing the organisation's institutional identity.
- The core job to be done is to move from civilisational-intellectual purpose into organised institutional action: review, publish, coordinate, communicate, and track work across roles.

### Brand Personality
- Scholarly
- Rooted
- Disciplined
- Institutional
- The interface should evoke confidence, seriousness, cultural continuity, and calm operational control.

### Aesthetic Direction
- The product should feel like a contemporary Bharatiya intellectual institution, not a generic software dashboard.
- Use a dual-layer visual system:
  - Public/demo gateway surfaces can carry more atmosphere, symbolic geometry, and philosophical framing.
  - Internal workflow surfaces should be calmer, clearer, and more editorial, while still sharing the same institutional DNA.
- Primary visual language:
  - warm parchment and off-white bases
  - deep navy/ink structure
  - saffron-led accents
  - restrained supporting colours by module
- Typography should feel editorial and dignified, with bilingual English/Hindi treatment where English explains and Hindi deepens.
- Support both light and dark themes, but light mode remains the primary visual reference for design decisions.

### Design Principles
- Civilisational in tone, operational in structure.
- Institutional gravitas and operational clarity should stay balanced roughly 50/50.
- Every screen should guide the user in this order where possible: identity cue, context cue, main action, evidence/data, deeper detail.
- Buttons, labels, and section headings should sound like the organisation's work, not generic startup SaaS language.
- Preserve bilingual intentionality: English for immediate comprehension, Hindi for grounding and resonance.
- Administrative surfaces should make authority, scope, and consequences legible before action.
- Avoid visual and tonal drift into:
  - spiritual app aesthetics
  - festival poster styling
  - startup dashboard conventions
