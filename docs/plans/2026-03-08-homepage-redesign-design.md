# Homepage Redesign Design

## Goal

Redesign the public homepage so first-time visitors immediately understand what Pragya Pravah is, why its work matters now, and how different people can engage. The page must combine clarity for newcomers with strong cultural-emotional force, while keeping the design and button language aligned with the organisation's philosophical and institutional character.

## Current Problem

The current homepage is visually rich but overly dense. It presents many ideas, motifs, and content blocks in quick succession, which weakens first-time comprehension. Visitors can sense cultural weight, but the page does not guide them cleanly through identity, relevance, and next steps.

The redesign should fix that by reducing cognitive noise, sharpening hierarchy, and making every section serve a distinct purpose.

## Audience

The homepage must work for all three of these first-time visitor types:

- curious general visitors
- intellectuals, speakers, and institutional stakeholders
- potential organisers or volunteers

Because all three matter, the page cannot optimise for only one conversion path. It needs a layered reading flow that introduces the organisation once, then offers tailored entry points.

## Core Principle

`clarity first, depth second, symbolism throughout`

Implications:

- the page should explain the organisation before asking users to admire it
- symbolism should reinforce meaning, not replace it
- every CTA, card, and section label should sound like Pragya Pravah, not like a generic product or NGO site
- English should aid first-pass comprehension, while Hindi deepens cultural and emotional resonance

## Narrative Structure

The homepage should follow this sequence:

1. `Hero Gateway`
- explain what Pragya Pravah is
- explain why the work matters now
- provide three primary actions immediately

2. `Institutional Framing`
- define Pragya Pravah as a Bharat-centric intellectual and organisational forum
- reduce ambiguity for new visitors

3. `Fields of Work`
- show active workstreams and aayams as living institutional functions
- move away from symbolic labels without explanation

4. `Why This Work Matters Now`
- connect philosophy to contemporary stakes
- explain relevance in discourse, knowledge, society, and public life

5. `Choose Your Path`
- provide clear routes for visitor, thinker, and organiser
- each path should have a tailored explanation and action

6. `Mission Into Operations`
- connect philosophy with action by showing that the organisation runs on disciplined processes and visible work
- bridge into the demo console and internal systems

7. `Closing Institutional CTA`
- leave the visitor with one clear sense of direction and one final action cluster

## Hero Strategy

The hero must do three things within the first 5 seconds:

- state what Pragya Pravah is
- state why it matters today
- offer meaningful next actions

This means the hero cannot be a purely poetic banner and cannot be a plain SaaS-style product header.

Recommended hero composition:

- one strong bilingual headline focused on institutional identity
- one concise relevance statement grounded in present-day discourse and civilisational continuity
- one structured 3-button action row
- one supporting visual field using seals, mandala geometry, manuscript texture, or disciplined symbolic patterning

## Button Philosophy

Buttons must not sound generic. Each one should speak directly to the organisation's work.

Buttons should function like institutional directives, not conversion clichés.

Good examples:

- `Understand the Vision / दृष्टि समझें`
- `Enter Demo Console / डेमो प्रणाली खोलें`
- `Connect with the Network / संवाद से जुड़ें`
- `Explore the Aayams / आयाम देखें`
- `Read Current Vimarsh / वर्तमान विमर्श पढ़ें`
- `See Organisational Work / कार्यप्रवाह देखें`

Avoid:

- Learn More
- Get Started
- Discover Now
- Explore More
- Contact Us as a generic primary CTA

## Visual Direction

The homepage should feel like a contemporary civilisational institution.

### Mood

- warm, atmospheric, serious
- spacious and easier to scan than the current homepage
- emotionally resonant without becoming decorative clutter

### Colour

- parchment and warm-white base
- deep ink and navy-charcoal for authority
- saffron as the primary active accent
- limited secondary accents used intentionally, not section-by-section rainbow styling

### Typography

- expressive, editorial heading system
- institutional, legible body type
- Devanagari used as an equal part of the page language
- heading cadence should feel declarative and civilisational, not startup-marketing-led

### Motifs

Use:

- seals
- sutra dividers
- disciplined mandala geometry
- manuscript-inspired framing
- lattice or archival patterns as subtle atmospherics

Avoid:

- over-layered symbolism in every section
- poster-style saturation
- purely devotional aesthetics
- interchangeable SaaS gradients and cards

## Bilingual Language System

The homepage must be intentionally bilingual.

Rule:

`English explains, Hindi deepens`

How this should work:

- English provides immediate clarity for new visitors
- Hindi adds civilisational grounding and emotional resonance
- hero and section headings should use paired language treatment
- not every paragraph needs to be duplicated in full if it harms readability
- buttons should be bilingual where possible, but remain concise

This preserves comprehension while ensuring the page still feels culturally native.

## Component Architecture

### 1. Hero Gateway

Contains:

- institutional headline
- present-day relevance statement
- three primary actions
- supporting symbolic visual

### 2. Institutional Card Band

Three quick-read cards explaining:

- the organisation's intellectual role
- its organisational function
- its Bharatiya knowledge orientation

### 3. Aayam Work Grid

Each tile should describe real work, not just titles.

Examples:

- Vimarsh: discourse and narrative engagement
- Shodh: research and knowledge work
- Prachar: outreach and public amplification
- Yuva: leadership formation

### 4. Why Now Section

A more forceful section tying the institution's philosophy to current reality.

This is the emotional-intellectual rise in the page narrative.

### 5. Audience Path Cards

Three cards:

- visitor
- thinker
- organiser

Each card should answer:

- why this person should care
- what they should do next

### 6. Mission Into Operations

A bridge section that shows the organisation is not only philosophical but also operationally disciplined.

This section should naturally support the `Enter Demo Console` path.

### 7. Closing Sutra / Footer CTA

End with a concise institutional sign-off and the strongest final CTA cluster.

## Responsive Behaviour

The redesign must remain strong on mobile.

Requirements:

- hero should stack cleanly without losing the 3-action logic
- visual symbolism should simplify rather than crush the layout
- bilingual hierarchy must remain readable on smaller screens
- audience path cards and workstream tiles should remain easy to scan with thumb-friendly CTAs

## Non-Goals

- no redesign of login or dashboard as part of this specific homepage task
- no auth changes
- no API or schema changes
- no attempt to put the entire organisation history on the first screen
- no generic marketing-site redesign disconnected from Pragya Pravah's identity

## Success Criteria

The redesign succeeds if:

- a first-time visitor can understand the organisation within seconds
- the page feels unmistakably Bharatiya and institutionally serious
- all three audience types see a credible next step
- the CTA labels sound native to the organisation's mission
- the homepage is more coherent, more memorable, and more usable than the current version
