# Project Mandates

## Design Context

### Users
The primary users are members of the Pragya Pravah organization, ranging from senior stakeholders (Vibhag/Prant levels) to daily operators (Unit heads, Karyakartas). Senior stakeholders focus on regional oversight and final approvals, while operators focus on event planning, thematic review, and public outreach.

### Brand Personality
The brand is **Civilisational, Institutional, and Disciplined**. It establishes a contemporary Bharatiya intellectual presence, balancing deep philosophical roots with modern operational rigour. It avoids generic "SaaS polish" in favour of scholarly gravitas and organizational seriousness.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Visual Tone**: Scholarly, rooted, and national. Uses a "manuscript-meets-console" aesthetic.
- **Color Palette**: Neutrals (parchment, stone, deep ink) with Saffron as the primary action color. Secondary accents like copper, maroon, and peacock blue are used for specific modules or states.
- **Typography**: Editorial English typography paired with dignified, legible Devanagari (Baloo 2 / Noto Sans).
- **Motifs**: Geometric seals, sutra dividers, mandala geometry, and lattice backgrounds.
- **Theme**: Supports both light and dark modes, with a warm, atmospheric feel.

### Design Principles
1. **Civilisational in Tone, Operational in Structure**: Every screen must establishes cultural identity through typography and motifs without sacrificing the speed and clarity required for institutional work.
2. **Identity-First Reading Order**: Screens follow a strict hierarchy: `identity cue -> context cue -> primary action -> evidence/data -> deeper detail`.
3. **Bilingual Depth**: English provides immediate operational clarity; Hindi (Devanagari) adds civilisational grounding and emotional resonance.
4. **Institutional Artefacts**: UI components like cards and panels should feel like serious internal records or scholarly manuscripts, not generic web widgets.
5. **Disciplined Motion**: Fast, tight transitions for internal operational screens; slower, atmospheric reveals for public-facing gateway surfaces.
